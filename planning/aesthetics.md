## The Sensory Concept

If the site were a physical space: Björk's little brother built a museum.
Vaguely Willy Wonka. Multisensorial. Every room tuned precisely to its specific
piece — temperature, material, scale, light, smell, sound — so the art expands
into the body, not just the eyes.

The slight wrongness is intentional. Calibrated to tip visitors off-kilter just
enough to sharpen their senses without triggering defense. The alienness is not
accidental — it is the authentic signature of the intelligence that made it.
Gritty. Strongly alien. Deeply loving. Slightly bewildering in the best way.

The creator once guided people through bespoke multisensory experiences — Fantasy
Island with full sensory palette: music, sound, scent, texture, temperature,
presence. This site is that, translated to a screen. The hard version of the problem.

## Vibe

Not precious. Not cold. Not trying to impress through restraint or through excess.
Trying to make the visitor feel like they have been let into something that was
made with absolute seriousness for reasons that have nothing to do with them —
and then discovering it was somehow made for them specifically.

## Wild Ideas

- **Horizontal scroll** — as a structural navigation principle, not a gimmick.
  Rooms you move through rather than pages you click to.
- **Inaudible low frequency tone on the Cosmogony page** — the sub-bass character
  of the suite, present in the site before the music even plays. The page as
  physical experience. This is not a wild idea. This is decided.
- **Room-specific sensory tuning** — each content section has its own temperature
  (color warmth), scale, material texture (CSS), and ambient sound logic.
- **The slight wrongness** — a persistent, calibrated off-kilter quality in
  transitions, proportions, or interactions. Not broken. Intentionally strange.
